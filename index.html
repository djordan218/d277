Welcome to the state of Oklahoma! Known for its rich history and diverse culture, Oklahoma offers a variety of attractions and experiences. Here are some interesting facts about the state:

Oklahoma Facts

Oklahoma is bordered by Colorado, Kansas, Missouri, Arkansas, Texas, and New Mexico. The Red River forms nearly all of its southern boundary; the Arkansas River flows across northeastern Oklahoma. Its highest point is Black Mesa (4,973 ft [1,516 m]), located in the Panhandle.

Key Statistics

  • Population: Approximately 4 million (as of 2024)
  • State Capital: Oklahoma City
  • Area: 69,899 square miles
  • State Bird: Scissor-tailed Flycatcher
  • State Flower: Oklahoma Rose
  • State Tree: Redbud

Historical Highlights

  1. 1907: Oklahoma became the 46th state of the United States.
  2. 1930s: The Dust Bowl significantly impacted Oklahoma's agricultural sector.
  3. 1995: The Oklahoma City bombing, a tragic event that led to significant changes in national security policies.
